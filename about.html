RealPage Rent Impact Dashboard

Ahmad Allaou, Peter Benzoni, Chelle Davies, Tim Majidzadeh & Patrick Yim

Is there a statistically significant change in rent level and / or housing supply associated with RealPage market share?

Abstract

This study investigates whether changes in rent levels and housing supply are statistically associated with RealPage's market share in multifamily property management. Drawing on recent debates and litigation surrounding the role of algorithmic pricing tools in rental markets, we examine whether regions with a higher adoption rate of RealPage software experience different rent trajectories or supply-side responses than comparable areas. Using a panel of metropolitan-level data on apartment rents, new building permits, and RealPage adoption over multiple years, we employ econometric models (e.g., fixed effects regressions) to control for confounding factors such as population growth, overall market trends, and local economic conditions. Our findings will shed light on whether RealPage's expansion correlates with measurable shifts in rent levels—or if it reflects broader market pressures already in play. By evaluating how market concentration and technological tools interact with housing supply and rental prices, this research aims to contribute to policy discussions around housing affordability, urban planning, and potential antitrust considerations. Ultimately, identifying any statistically significant link between RealPage's market share and housing market outcomes could inform stakeholders—from renters and landlords to policymakers—about the implications of consolidating pricing technology in an era of heightened housing instability, inflationary pressures, and homelessness.

Data Privacy Policy

RealPage Rent Impact Dashboard protects personal data by employing robust privacy safeguards, guided by Belmont Principles (respect, beneficence, and justice) for data handling, while leveraging frameworks like Solove's Taxonomy, Nissenbaum's Contextual Integrity, and Mulligan et al.'s Analytic. We anonymize data, secure it with controlled access, and perform regular audits and staff training to ensure compliance with industry standards. Our system only displays public property data, including addresses or zip codes—no PII is collected. Ongoing risk assessments and transparent policies reinforce stakeholder trust.
